We underestimate damage

done to the sky

when we allow words

to slip away

into the clouds.

<br>

I remember making promises

to you outside. We

were watching flowers

that hadn't opened.

A bee darted, careful

not to stick to

your half-shut mouth.

<br>

(1994)
